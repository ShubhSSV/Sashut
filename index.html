Plant Information


Plant Name: Sashut


Benefits: The Sashut plant is known for its medicinal and therapeutic properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine. It is often used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which aid in reducing inflammation and preventing cellular damage. Sashut leaves can be brewed into teas that are known to boost immunity and improve digestion. The plant also has a mild sedative effect, which can help alleviate stress and promote restful sleep. In skincare, its extracts are used in natural remedies for treating minor wounds, soothing skin irritations, and reducing signs of aging.



Season: The Sashut plant thrives in warm and humid climates, making the spring and summer seasons ideal for its growth and development. It flourishes in regions with a moderate to high level of sunlight and can sustain growth during mild autumns as well. Although it may continue to grow during winter in tropical areas, its growth rate significantly slows down in cooler temperatures. For optimal yield, Sashut is generally harvested at the end of summer, allowing it to achieve full maturity and potency.



Region: Native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, the Sashut plant is widely found in countries such as India, Thailand, and Malaysia. It prefers well-drained, loamy soil and is commonly cultivated in rural areas where traditional herbal practices are still prevalent. In addition to its natural habitat, Sashut has adapted well to other tropical climates, allowing it to be grown in areas of Africa, Central America, and the Caribbean where similar environmental conditions exist.



Age: The Sashut plant is a perennial herb that can live for several years, typically reaching maturity within 2 to 3 years. Once mature, the plant can continuously produce leaves for harvest, given proper care and conditions. The average lifespan of a Sashut plant can extend up to 10 years or more, though the most potent leaves are generally harvested in the early years. Regular pruning and maintenance encourage new growth, allowing the plant to sustain productivity for a longer period.
